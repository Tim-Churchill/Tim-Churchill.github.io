The Knowledge Map

A short, text-only version of the idea.

The Core Idea

Learning should leave a map.

I'm building a platform that maps what you know, shows how your knowledge grows over time, and helps you decide what to learn next.

Think of it as a living knowledge map that updates from what you learn and share.

  • Full Project Page

The Problem

Learning is fragmented.

  • Notes, posts, and courses do not connect.
  • You cannot clearly see what you know.
  • You cannot measure progress in a meaningful way.

We spend thousands of hours learning, but we have no clear structure of what we have built.

The Solution

Track understanding, not just activity.

  • Build a personal knowledge map.
  • Track concepts and mastery over time.
  • Update the map from study, posts, and discussion.

Instead of tracking what you did, we track what you understand.

How It Works

Your activity builds the map.

1

Learn

Read, study, build, or discuss.

2

Log

Post or record the activity.

3

Map

The system links it to concepts.

4

Grow

Your knowledge map gains depth over time.

Why It Stands Out

You do not just track learning. You design it.

The map can support planning, exploration, and comparison instead of acting like a passive archive.

Plan future topics Explore strengths and gaps Compare knowledge structure

Why It Matters

Make learning visible, structured, and cumulative.

This turns learning into something you can see, measure, and shape instead of something that disappears into scattered artifacts.
