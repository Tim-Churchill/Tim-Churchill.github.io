The Knowledge Map

A fast, visual version of the concept for judges, classmates, or anyone who needs the big picture quickly.

01 | The Core Idea

Map what you know. Watch it grow. Decide what to learn next.

I'm building a platform that maps what you know, shows how your knowledge grows over time, and helps you decide what to learn next.

Think of it as a living map of your knowledge that updates automatically based on what you learn and share.

  • Full Project Page
  • How It Works

30-Second Version

I'm building a platform that maps what you know and tracks how your understanding grows over time. Right now learning is fragmented. Notes, posts, and courses do not connect. This creates a structured knowledge graph from your activity, showing what you've learned, how deeply you understand it, and what to learn next. It turns learning into something visible, measurable, and intentional.

02 | The Problem

Learning is fragmented, but understanding is not.

  • Notes live in one place.
  • Posts live somewhere else.
  • Courses live somewhere else again.
  • You cannot clearly see what you actually know.
  • You cannot measure progress in a meaningful way.
  • You do not know what to learn next.
  • Social platforms reward content, not understanding.

"We spend thousands of hours learning, but we have no clear structure of what we've built."

03 | The Solution

Track understanding instead of activity logs.

  • Build a personal knowledge map.
  • Organize concepts into a structured graph.
  • Track mastery over time.
  • Update automatically from posts, study activity, and discussions.

"Instead of tracking what you did, we track what you understand."

05 | The Knowledge Map

You can literally see the shape of your knowledge.

Each node represents a concept.

Connections show how topics relate to each other.

The graph shows what you have explored, how deeply you understand it, and how ideas connect.

04 | How It Works

Your activity directly builds a structured representation of your knowledge.

1

You learn something

Read, study, build, or discuss.

2

You post or log it

The activity becomes usable signal, not just a record.

3

The system interprets it

It identifies related concepts and updates the relevant parts of the graph.

4

Your map gets richer

The structure grows more detailed as your understanding accumulates over time.

06 | Mastery And Progress

Progress is not streaks. It is evolving understanding.

Each concept can move through visible levels of mastery rather than sitting in a binary learned or not learned state.

Beginner Intermediate Advanced Integrated

"Progress is how your understanding evolves."

07 | Planning

You do not just track learning. You design it.

Future topics can exist on the map before you know them. Intention turns into visible structure.

  • Build learning plans directly on the map.
  • Add future nodes before mastery exists.
  • Create routes instead of vague goals.

08 | Explore Mode

See your learning from different angles.

Explore mode helps people understand the pattern of how they have been learning, not just what they last touched.

Recent Activity Dense Areas Weak Areas

"It helps you understand how you've been learning."

09 | Compare Mode

Compare knowledge structure, not just output.

People could compare their map with friends, benchmarks, or reference figures and get insight into overlap, gaps, and strengths.

  • Where do we overlap?
  • What am I missing?
  • Where is my depth unusually strong?

"You can compare your knowledge structure, not just your output."

10 | Why This Matters

This turns learning into something you can see, measure, and shape.

The goal is to make learning visible, structured, and cumulative. When people can see the shape of their knowledge, they can learn more intentionally, spot gaps earlier, and stay motivated by real progress instead of empty motion.

Visible Structured Cumulative Intentional

11 | What Makes It Unique

It is not a note app, a course platform, or a social feed.

Not a note app Not a course platform Not a social feed
Knowledge graph Social system Planning tool

"There is not really anything else that combines structure, progress, and social learning like this."

12 | Future Vision

Over time, the system can guide what you should learn next.

  • Personalized learning paths.
  • Intelligent recommendations.
  • Benchmark comparisons.
  • Richer knowledge modeling.

The map starts as a mirror of learning and grows into a guide for it.
